You Know You Wanna Learn More

I currently work at a start-up in downtown Detroit, Grand Circus, that trains adults to be software developers. We also work with a few Detroit high schools where we teach students how to code and introduces them to careers in technology.

My passion, however, lies in introducing youth to opportunities that will set them on a path that will allow them to meet their full potential, regardless of background. Why? Well, I grew up in a single mom home in Seattle, the result of the early death of my father. Growing up poor, I was blessed to have a community that supported me and provided endless opportunities to build my brain. By the end of high school, I had the opportunity to attend a summer program at Phillips Exeter Academy, a prestigious boarding school in New Hampshire, travel throughout the country to compete in Mock Trial National championships and spend a summer on Stanford University’s campus learning from world class professors. In college, I studied African & African American Studies at Stanford University, a result of my summer on campus and the tremendous financial aid they offer to low-income students. I was involved in the Black community through my work at the Black Community Services Center and as a member of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc. I served the broader community as President of the Associated Students of Stanford University (the student government of undergrad & grad) and entertained the masses as a member of an R&B, Motown and Soul a cappella group, Everyday People. Since then, I’ve lived and worked in Vietnam as a teacher, ran a call center in Atlanta, introduced middle school students to future careers & college in Brooklyn and improved high school students academic knowledge & helped them get into college in Detroit.
